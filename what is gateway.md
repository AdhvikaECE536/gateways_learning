### Definition:

A gateway is a network node that acts as an entry and exit point between 2 networks, translating protocols, formats, or addressing schemes so that data can flow between systems that would otherwise be incompatible. 

It is present at the boundary of a network. Every packet that enters or leaves the local network passes through it.

### What it does:
• Connects two different network types

• Translates protocols between them

• Routes traffic in/out of a network

• Can filter, inspect, or modify packets

• Acts as a single controlled entry point

### What it is NOT

• Not just a router (routers work within same protocol families)

• Not just a switch (switches operate at L2)

• Not just a firewall (though gateways can include one)

• Not a repeater or hub
